# Template: Serbian/Bosnian Drill Track

**Best for**: drill, trap, dark club tracks  
**Hook formula**: Sound + action + brand/status tag  
**English target**: 15–25% of tokens (mostly tags, brands, and ad-libs)

## Intro (1–4 lines, optional)
Start with a producer tag, a laugh, or a dark sound cue.

> [Producer tag or sound cue]
> [Laughter / ad-lib]
> [Artist name] + [track name]

## Hook (4–8 lines, 2–5 tokens/line) — REPEAT FIRST
Drill hooks often open the track. Use repetition, onomatopoeia, or a short phrase.

> [Onomatopoeia / sound tag]\n
> [Action + consequence]\n
> [Action + consequence]\n
> [Rhythmic tag or brand reference]

## Verse 1 (16–24 lines, 4–7 tokens/line)
Keep the energy high. Short, punchy lines work best.

- Set the scene in a club or street.
- Use one brand or status object.
- Describe a girl/guy, a rival, or a crew.
- End with a threat or a boast.

> [Location] je pun, a ja sam tu\n
> [Brand] na meni, a [someone] gleda u\n
> [Action] kada [trigger]\n
> [Consequence]\n
> [Specific image]\n
> [Boast or warning]

## Pre-hook (2–4 lines)
Build up to the hook return.

> [Repetition]\n
> [Imperative]\n
> [Brand or status line]
