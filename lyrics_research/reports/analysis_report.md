# Serbian/Bosnian Tech-House/Pop Lyrics — Analysis Report

**Dataset**: 10 songs (Serbian 9, Bosnian 1)  
**Total lines**: 396 | **Total tokens**: 2,253 | **Avg type-token ratio**: 0.381 | **Avg English injection**: 10%

---

## Aggregate Findings

- **Repetition rules**: club-oriented tracks rely on heavy repetition (type-token ratios 0.28–0.39). "Elektroljubav" is the outlier at 0.58 — dense with technology references and fewer choruses.
- **Short lines dominate**: average line length is 4.6–8.1 tokens; 4.6–5.4 is the sweet spot for chant hooks.
- **English is seasoning, not main dish**: 2–30% English words, usually in hooks, interjections, or brand/cultural references.
- **Hook structure**: most choruses are 2–4 lines repeated 2–4 times; the strongest hooks use phonetic symmetry (da-da-da / mwah-mwah-mwah) or Balkan pride slogans.
- **Themes**: love/obsession, nightlife/club identity, Balkan pride, modern technology, self-mythology.

## What Makes a Good Serbian/Bosnian Club/Pop Lyric?

1. **Chantability over complexity**: the best hooks are 4–6 syllables, use repetition, and avoid rare words.
2. **Contradiction sells**: love/hate, rich/broke, hot/cold, present/absent — tension creates memorability.
3. **Balkan specificity**: references to *čaršija*, *rakija*, *Balkan*, *Slav*, *splav* give regional identity.
4. **English as punctuation**: English words are used for hooks, tech/culture references, and interjections, rarely for narrative.
5. **Sensory nouns**: gold, chains, asphalt, diamonds, perfume, heat, incense, computers — concrete objects carry abstract emotion.
6. **Repetition is architecture**: expect hooks to repeat 3–7 times; verses can be shorter and more imagistic.
