# Serbian/Bosnian Tech-House/Pop Lyric Style Guide

A compact field guide for writing club-ready Serbian/Bosnian pop lyrics based on the 10-song research corpus.

---

## 1. Structural Recipe

- **Total lines**: 30–50.
- **Verse**: 8–12 lines, 5–7 tokens per line, image-heavy.
- **Pre-chorus** (optional): 2–4 lines building tension.
- **Chorus**: 4–8 lines, heavy repetition, 4–6 tokens per line.
- **Bridge**: 4 lines, shift perspective or add a twist.
- **Outro**: repeat the hook or a stripped version.

### Hook placement rule
A hook line should appear at least 3 times in the song. The first appearance should be within the first 60 seconds of lyrics.

---

## 2. Line-Length Targets

| Section | Tokens per line | Purpose |
|---------|-----------------|---------|
| Verse | 5–7 | storytelling, imagery |
| Pre-chorus | 4–6 | tension, breathless build |
| Chorus | 3–5 | chant, singalong, dance floor |
| Bridge | 4–6 | contrast, emotional peak |

Avoid lines longer than 10 tokens unless used for comic/technological density (e.g., "Ja s mačetom igr'o često chess i četov'o").

---

## 3. Theme Palette

Use 1–2 primary themes and 1 secondary theme.

- **Love/obsession**: most common; pair with self-destruction, distance, or power imbalance.
- **Nightlife/club identity**: party as pride, party as escape, party as cultural statement.
- **Balkan pride**: regional references, language-as-identity, anti-melancholy optimism.
- **Modern technology/AI**: love through screens, generational labels, gaming/crypto metaphors.
- **Luxury/paranoia**: diamonds, gold, debt, insomnia, surveillance.

## 4. Vocabulary Clusters

### Core emotional words
- ljubav, volim, srce, greh, ruka, dodir, bol, želja, nevolja, pakao, raj

### Nightlife words
- žurka, klub, noć, mrak, splav, ples, ritam, glas, zvuk, rulja, čaršija

### Balkan identity words
- Balkan, Slav, srpski, bosanski, rakija, čaša, kafana, čaršija, brat, majka

### Material/technology words
- dijamant, zlato, lanac, asfalt, računar, ekran, tehnologija, inteligencija, bitcoin, igra

### Useful interjections
- ej, hej, ho, oh, yeah, u-yeah, aman, ma, zar ne, joj, hajde

---

## 5. Rhyme & Sound Patterns

- **End-rhyme schemes**: AABB or ABAB in verses; chorus lines often rhyme in pairs.
- **Assonance**: long *a* and *o* sounds dominate ("zlatom / ledeno", "asfalt / pakla").
- **Internal rhyme**: use within chorus lines for momentum ("Bruji, bruji čaršija").
- **Alliteration**: stack words with same initial sound for a hook ("fobije, magije, utopije, nostalgije").
- **Phonetic substitution**: replace English sounds with Serbian equivalents ("yes" → "da", "kiss" → "mwah").

---

## 6. Hook Formulas

### Formula A: Action + Object
> [Verb] [my body / my heart / the night], [verb], [object]

Example: *"Savij moje telo, savij origami"*

### Formula B: Identity Chant
> [I am / We are] [X] / [Y] / [Z]

Example: *"Ja sam glas iz računara / Telefonska industrija"*

### Formula C: Bilingual Phonetic Taunt
> [English phrase] / [Serbian correction]

Example: *"Nije yes, yes, yes / Nego da, da, da"*

### Formula D: Negation Loop
> [No, no, no] / [Consequence]

Example: *"Nema, nema, nema me / Gazim, za mnom gori put"*

### Formula E: Rumor/Atmosphere Loop
> [Repetition of environment] / [Who I am]

Example: *"Bruji, bruji čaršija / To je ona moja muškarčina"*

---

## 7. Poetic Devices That Work

- **Anaphora**: repeating the same word at the start of successive lines.
- **Paradox**: "I choose you / I don't need you".
- **Hyperbole**: "even if the road leads to hell".
- **Oxymoron**: "gold chain / cold heart".
- **Metonymy**: using *čaršija* (market) for gossip, *rakija* for energy.
- **Code-switching**: drop English words for tech, fashion, or crowd commands.

---

## 8. English Injection Rules

- Keep English under 20% of total tokens for most tracks; 30% only for explicit bilingual gimmicks.
- Use English for: interjections, tech terms, brand/culture references, hook sounds.
- Avoid English in narrative verses unless the character is code-switching naturally.

---

## 9. Quality Checklist

- [ ] Hook appears at least 3 times.
- [ ] Chorus lines are under 6 tokens each.
- [ ] At least one concrete sensory noun per verse.
- [ ] One regional or cultural reference somewhere in the song.
- [ ] A contradiction or paradox in the hook or pre-chorus.
- [ ] Repetition feels intentional, not accidental.
- [ ] The last line of the chorus is the most memorable phrase.
