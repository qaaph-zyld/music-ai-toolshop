# Serbian/Bosnian Hip-Hop/Drill Lyric Style Guide

A field guide for writing Bosnian/Serbian hip-hop, trap, and drill lyrics based on the 10-song rap research corpus.

---

## 1. Structural Recipe

- **Total lines**: 40–70 (rap tracks are longer than pop tracks because of feature verses).
- **Intro**: 1–4 bars, often a producer tag or a spoken hook ("Je l' ovo Popov?").
- **Verse 1**: 16–24 lines, 5–8 tokens/line, narrative or boast-heavy.
- **Hook/Chorus**: 4–8 lines, 4–6 tokens/line, repeated 3–4 times.
- **Verse 2**: 16–24 lines (often a feature artist with a different perspective).
- **Bridge/Outro**: 2–4 lines, stripped hook or ad-lib fade.

### Hook placement rule
The hook should appear within the first 60 seconds and at least 3 times total. Drill hooks often start the song.

---

## 2. Line-Length Targets

| Section | Tokens per line | Purpose |
|---------|-----------------|---------|
| Verse | 5–8 | storytelling, boasts, scene-setting |
| Pre-hook | 4–6 | build, breath pause |
| Hook | 3–5 | chant, mosh-pit, singalong |
| Bridge | 4–6 | contrast, emotional turn |

Trap and drill allow shorter, punchier lines (2–4 tokens) when the beat is half-time.

---

## 3. Theme Palette

Use 1–2 primary themes and 1 secondary theme.

- **Money/status**: brands, cars, clubs, cash, jewelry, luxury travel.
- **Love/sex/transaction**: desire, possession, betrayal, indifference, club seduction.
- **Street/city persona**: Belgrade/Bosnian neighborhoods, specific clubs, walking, night life.
- **Regional pride/identity**: Balkan, Slavic, Serbian/Bosnian dialect, leaving vs. staying.
- **Satire/commentary**: mocking hypocrisy, new money, gender politics, fame.

---

## 4. Vocabulary Clusters

### Core identity words
- ja, mi, moj, tvoj, bebo, mala, decko, riba, kume, brat, majka, tata

### Money/status words
- love, para, keš, soma, sef, VVS, diamant, zlato, lancic, torba, revija, enterijer

### Brand names (use as adjectives/scenery)
- Gucci, Dior, Fendi, Vuitton, Louis, Balenciaga, Paciotti, Benza, BMW, Supreme, Kenzo, Cîroca, Martini, Red Bull, Paulaner, FaceTime

### Nightlife/location words
- klub, žurka, splav, čaršija, Taš, Tašmajdan, Beograd, Sarajevo, Balkan, Sava, Dunav, mrak, noć, bine, šank

### Drill/hip-hop tags
- vroom, gangsta, bitch, mode, switch, broke, nasty, squad, drill, trap, amor, ave, choky, felna, brena, wannabe, doggy, tarantino

### Useful interjections
- ej, bre, tebra, joj-joj, woo-woo, ha, je, ooh-la-la, e-e-e-e, mmm, izvini, ma

---

## 5. Flow & Rhyme Patterns

- **End-rhyme schemes**: AABB in choruses; ABAB or internal rhymes in verses.
- **Internal rhyme**: stack rhymes within the same line for speed and density.
- **Assonance**: long *a* and *o* sounds dominate drill; short *e* and *i* for faster flows.
- **Alliteration**: use same initial sound for a hook ("Nadrkano hodanje, gazim limenke").
- **Ad-libs**: place short sounds or repeated words after lines ("je", "e", "woo-woo", "m-m").
- **Code-switching**: drop English or pseudo-English phrases for tags and brands.

---

## 6. Hook Formulas

### Formula A: Brand + Status
> [Brand list] + [I am / she is] [status phrase]

Example: *"Louis (Vuitton), Fendi, prava dama / Đavo na ramenu mi spava"*

### Formula B: Sound + Action
> [Onomatopoeia] + [what happens] + [consequence]

Example: *"Vroom, kad me opije, glumi da je stena / Kukove uvije kad krene Makarena"*

### Formula C: Regional Chant
> [chant syllable] + [Balkan/regional statement]

Example: *"Ave, ave, a Balkan daje sve / Tu sam rođen, ali morô sam da nestanem"*

### Formula D: Definition Hook
> [We are X] / [everyone talks about X] / [but nobody knows X]

Example: *"Mi smo ta dilema / Glavna tabo tema, a o nama ništa nema"*

### Formula E: Walking/Action Mantra
> [Action phrase] + [where / how] + [I am, you are not]

Example: *"Nadrkano hodanje, gazim limenke Red bula / Izlazim sa žurke u stilu, ja fensi, ti nula"*

---

## 7. Poetic Devices That Work

- **Anaphora**: repeat the same phrase at the start of successive lines ("Ova mala u redu je").
- **Brand synecdoche**: use a luxury object to stand for a whole lifestyle.
- **Hyperbole**: "pogled leden kao vrh Himalaja", "srce komad leda".
- **Metonymy**: "Balkan" for the regional lifestyle, "Taš" for Belgrade nightlife.
- **Profanity as punctuation**: strong words can mark emotional peaks, not just shock.
- **Toponymy**: name specific streets, clubs, or cities to ground the track.

---

## 8. English Injection Rules

- Keep English under 15% for most tracks; 20% only if the hook is built on English tags.
- Use English for: brand names, drill tags, ad-libs, tech references, short exclamations.
- Avoid English in narrative verses unless the character naturally code-switches.
- Pseudo-English or nonsense chants ("En-den-dini-sava-raka-tini") are valid drill texture.

---

## 9. Dialect & Regional Notes

- **Serbian lyrics** often use Ekavian ("bele", "reč", "noć").
- **Bosnian lyrics** often use Ijekavian ("bijelo", "riječ", "noć" is shared) and more Turkish loanwords.
- Use regional slang sparingly: *bre*, *tebra*, *kume*, *joj*, *aman*, *jebote*.
- A mix of Serbian and Bosnian artists in one project gives a broader regional picture.

---

## 10. Quality Checklist

- [ ] Hook appears at least 3 times and is chantable.
- [ ] At least one concrete brand, car, club, or neighborhood reference.
- [ ] One contrast (rich/poor, love/hate, stay/leave, soft/hard).
- [ ] Verse lines are denser than chorus lines.
- [ ] Ad-libs or interjections feel intentional, not random.
- [ ] The last hook line is the most memorable phrase.
- [ ] Dialect matches the artist's region.
